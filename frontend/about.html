About

🧬 HelixMatch

A dynamic programming-based pre-screening tool that assesses genetic disease risk by comparing DNA sequences against curated libraries of known pathogenic mutations. It narrows the diagnostic space before expensive confirmatory testing.

The Problem

Hereditary diseases affect roughly 1 in 17 people globally, with 80% of rare diseases having genetic origins. Yet commercial DNA kits cost $100–$2,000—out of reach for populations in low-income countries where GNI per capita is below $1,000. This diagnostic inequity, combined with the fact that most causative mutations are well-characterized and publicly documented, motivated an algorithmic pre-screening alternative that reduces cost barriers without sacrificing detection sensitivity.

The Solution: Dynamic Programming

A naive approach to DNA comparison enumerates all possible alignments—an exponential operation infeasible for real genetic data. Dynamic programming collapses this by breaking the problem into overlapping subproblems and storing results in a scoring matrix.

An intractable search becomes a deterministic O(m × n) operation, guaranteeing optimal local alignment with modest computational resources.

Smith-Waterman Algorithm

HelixMatch employs Smith-Waterman, designed specifically for local sequence alignment. Unlike global methods, it identifies the highest-scoring matching subsequence—ideal for short hereditary mutations embedded within longer genomes.

H[i][j] = max(0, H[i-1][j-1] + match/mismatch, H[i-1][j] + gap, H[i][j-1] + gap)

Key Features:

  • Zero floor discards poorly matching regions, keeping only locally similar subsequences
  • Gap penalties detect insertions, deletions, and point mutations
  • Traceback from the highest-scoring cell extracts the optimal matching subsequence

Output & Clinical Application

Raw alignment scores are converted into meaningful metrics via position-based ranking, where mutation location carries differential clinical weight. HelixMatch outputs a confidence percentage per referenced disease, enabling early detection before costly confirmatory tests.

HelixMatch is an early detection instrument for research contexts. It does not replace clinical diagnosis, rather it precedes it by flagging potential risks algorithmically so targeted confirmatory tests are ordered only where evidence supports them. This narrows the diagnostic space, reduces reliance on broad costly panels, and helps make genetic screening accessible where it is needed most.
